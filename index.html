Campaign Bucket Playbook: How to Read & React

For each campaign bucket, here's what healthy looks like, what deterioration signals look like, and what levers to pull. Use this to prep for client calls and guide daily optimization.

🛡️ Branded Search Low maintenance
Goal Defend the SERP. Make sure Ten Thousand is visible when people search the brand name. This is not incremental — these people were coming anyway. We fund it to keep competitors (Vuori, Rhone, Lululemon) from stealing the click.
Healthy looks like Impression Share >90%. Low CPC ($0.50–$1.50). Spend is stable and predictable. ROAS will always look insanely high (20x+) — do not use this as a benchmark for anything else.
Deterioration signals Impression Share drops below 85% — a competitor is bidding harder on the brand name.
CPC spikes >$2 — competitive pressure or auction dynamics shifting.
Spend suddenly increases 30%+ — could be a competitor running a conquest campaign on our brand terms.
Levers to pull Devesh: If IS drops, increase bids slightly — don't let competitors steal the top spot. If CPC spikes, check auction insights for who's bidding. If a new competitor appears, flag to Balaji.
Jackie: Ensure branded ad copy is strong and updated — sitelinks, callouts, promotion extensions all current.
Client call framing "Brand defense is stable at 94% impression share. No competitive threats to flag." Keep it short — execs don't need a deep dive here unless something is wrong.
🚀 Non-Brand Search High priority — scale lever
Goal This is our most incremental channel. People searching "men's training shorts" or "best workout pants" have no brand preference yet. Every conversion here is likely a genuinely new customer. Juice ROAS and find ways to scale.
Healthy looks like ROAS 2x+ (currently fluctuating 1.2–3x). Conversion volume growing or stable WoW. Search terms are relevant — not polluted with brand queries leaking in.
Deterioration signals ROAS drops below 1.5x for two consecutive weeks — we're spending more than we're getting back.
Conversion volume drops while spend stays flat — the audience is tapped or ad relevance is declining.
Search term report shows branded queries — leakage inflating ROAS. We caught this before and cleaned it up. Check monthly.
CPC inflating >15% MoM — competition is heating up or quality scores are dropping.
Levers to pull Devesh: Run search term audit weekly. Add negatives proactively. If ROAS is dropping, check if it's a CPC problem (auction) or a CVR problem (landing page / audience). Shift budget toward keyword themes that are converting. Test tROAS bid strategies with conservative targets first.
Jackie: Test new ad copy angles — product-specific benefits, seasonal hooks, social proof. Ensure RSA pin strategy is tight (brand name in H1, product in H2, CTA in H3).
Together: If a specific product category is winning in non-brand (e.g., "men's 7 inch shorts"), flag to Balaji to allocate more budget and potentially create dedicated campaigns for that theme.
Client call framing "Non-brand ROAS came in at 2.2x this week, up from 1.8x. We added 12 negatives from the search term audit and shifted $X from [underperformer] to [winner]. Conversion volume was [up/down] — here's why." This is the bucket Patrick cares about most for growth. Always lead with ROAS, then explain the story.
🛒 Shopping (Non-Brand) Fix in progress — feed is the lever
Goal Scale Shopping into a real revenue driver. Currently underperforming because the feed is broken — miscategorized products, missing attributes, no custom labels. The supplemental feed we built should fix this. Post-upload, this is the biggest upside campaign bucket.
Healthy looks like ROAS 3x+ (aspirational — currently 1.2x). Products appearing in correct categories. Shorts queries show shorts, not t-shirts. OOS products suppressed. High impression share on hero products.
Deterioration signals ROAS below 1.5x after feed upload — feed fix didn't move the needle, deeper issues at play.
High click volume but no conversions on specific products — likely OOS products still showing, or product pages not converting.
Impression share declining — competitors outbidding or feed quality score still low.
Products miscategorized in Merchant Center — supplemental feed not applying correctly, check for ID mismatches.
Levers to pull Devesh: After supplemental feed upload, track ROAS daily for 2 weeks to measure before/after impact. Use custom labels to create product-group bid adjustments — bid higher on hero products (Interval shorts), lower on low-margin or low-volume items. Check for OOS products eating clicks (this was 59% of the feed before). Monitor search term overlap with non-brand search to avoid internal competition.
Jackie: Coordinate with Patrick's team when new products launch — notify Balaji so the supplemental feed gets updated. Ensure product images in the feed are strong (lifestyle shots > flat lays for Shopping).
Together: If Shopping starts scaling, this becomes the single biggest opportunity. Track it closely post-feed-upload and report before/after to the client with concrete numbers.
Client call framing Pre-feed upload: "Shopping is still underperforming at 1.2x ROAS. We have the supplemental feed ready — once uploaded, we expect material improvement from proper categorization, custom labels, and OOS suppression. We'll report before/after."
Post-feed upload: "Since the feed went live on [date], Shopping ROAS has moved from 1.2x to [X]. Here's the product-level breakdown of what's working."
⚠️ Performance Max Caution — junk traffic risk
Goal PMax is Google's black box. It can serve across Search, Shopping, Display, YouTube, Gmail, and Discover — but we can't control where. The risk is that it serves junk traffic (Display, Discover) that looks like conversions but doesn't drive real business. Hold to a high ROAS bar. Treat with skepticism.
Healthy looks like ROAS 4x+ (we need it high because we can't verify quality). Conversions are backed up by Shopify data (not just platform-reported). Search term insights show relevant queries, not brand leakage.
Deterioration signals ROAS below 3x — at this level with PMax opacity, we're likely losing money on true incrementality.
PMax "Themes" campaign at 0.9x ROAS — already flagged, actively losing money. Needs to be killed or heavily restructured.
Search term insights show brand queries — PMax is cannibalizing branded traffic and inflating its own ROAS. We've caught this before.
Large gap between platform-reported conversions and Shopify orders — PMax is counting view-through or assisted conversions that aren't real purchases.
Levers to pull Devesh: Run the search term insights report weekly (it's limited but it's all we get). Cross-reference PMax conversions against Shopify to validate. If ROAS drops below 3x, reduce budget immediately — don't wait for it to recover. Add brand exclusions if Google allows. Use asset group reporting to see which creative/audience combos are performing.
Jackie: Keep asset groups clean — strong creative, clear product focus per asset group. PMax creative quality directly affects where Google serves the ads (better creative = more Search/Shopping, worse creative = more Display/Discover junk).
Escalate to Balaji: Any ROAS below 2x or evidence of brand cannibalization. PMax decisions often have strategic implications (kill vs. restructure vs. accept).
Client call framing "PMax is at [X] ROAS. We validated against Shopify and [it checks out / there's a gap]. We're maintaining a high ROAS bar here because of limited transparency. [Action taken: reduced budget / restructured asset groups / killed underperformer]." Patrick is skeptical of PMax too — be honest about the limitations. They respect transparency here.
📺 Video / Demand Gen Experimental — top of funnel
Goal Top of funnel awareness and audience building. We recently restructured into two audience buckets: Runners (marathon, trail, Strava/AllTrails signals) and Hybrid Performance (CrossFit, functional fitness, Spartan/Tough Mudder). Low ROAS is expected and okay — we're measuring engagement, reach, and whether it feeds the mid/bottom funnel over time.

Deterioration signals Engagement rates (VCR, CTR) drop significantly — creative fatigue or audience mismatch.
Frequency exceeds 3–4x on the same audience — we're annoying people, not reaching new ones.
Zero assisted conversions after 3+ weeks — audience targeting may be too broad or too irrelevant.
Spend accelerates without proportional reach growth — diminishing returns, audience is saturated.
Levers to pull Jackie (primary owner here): This is Jackie's strongest zone. Monitor engagement by audience segment — are Runners or Hybrid performing better? Compare Shorts creative vs. Pants creative performance in dedicated ad groups.
Devesh: Track assisted conversion path data. Set up a 14-day and 30-day lookback comparison. If one audience segment is showing downstream conversions and the other isn't, shift budget. Monitor frequency caps. Flag if spend is growing without reach growth.
Together: This is the "long game" bucket. Set expectations with the client that direct ROAS will always be low. The story to tell is engagement quality + downstream lift.
Client call framing "Demand Gen restructure is in learning phase. Runners audience is showing [X] VCR, Hybrid is at [Y]. We're seeing early engagement signals on [product type]. We'll have cleaner reads in [X] days as the learning phase completes." Patrick said he's excited to see audience-level reporting here. Give him the granularity he asked for — Runners vs. Hybrid, Shorts vs. Pants creative performance.
🧪 Experimental (Snap / X / New Channel Ramp) Contained — exec communication is critical
Goal Test new channels with contained budgets. Right now this is Snap (and soon X if the credits deal closes). When Meta comes back online, the first 7–14 days of ramp-up should also be treated as experimental. The #1 priority here is not performance — it's making sure experimental spend doesn't spook the execs by inflating the blended pCPA.
Healthy looks like Experimental spend stays under 10-15% of total marketing spend. pCPA trends down over 7–14 day windows (not day-over-day). Platform is learning and showing signs of settling. We have a clear "what we're testing and why" narrative for the client.
Deterioration signals Experimental % of total spend creeps above 15% — containment is failing, pull back budget.
pCPA not improving after 14 days — the channel may not work for this brand. Pause and reassess.
Weekend spend spikes — Snap burned us here before. Weekend budget spikes drove pCPA up and triggered client questions. Set day-parting or budget caps.
No halo effect on Shopify new customers — if Snap is spending but Shopify new customer count isn't moving, the traffic isn't converting.
Levers to pull Devesh: Set weekly budget caps, not just daily. Monitor weekend delivery closely (this is where Snap blew up last time). Track the 7-day and 14-day rolling pCPA, not the daily number. If pCPA hasn't improved after 14 days at current spend level, recommend pausing to Balaji.
Jackie: Snap creative is a different beast — vertical, fast, snackable. Test multiple hooks in the first 3 seconds. Coordinate with Devesh on which creative formats are getting the best cost-per-view and cost-per-click.
Together before client call: Always prep the experimental narrative in advance. "We spent $X on experimental this week (Y% of total). Here's what we learned. Here's what we adjusted. Here's the impact on blended pCPA and why it's contained."

Blended pCPA was $96, but Evergreen pCPA was $48 — the core business is healthy. We're monitoring the halo effect on Shopify new customers." Always separate experimental from core. Always show containment. Always give the "Evergreen is fine" safety blanket alongside the blended number.

Creative Performance Triage: The 4-Bucket Framework

Not every ad needs to drive purchases to be valuable. Some ads fill the remarketing pool with warm audiences that convert later through other ads. This framework gives Jackie and Devesh a shared language for evaluating creative and deciding what to keep, scale, rotate, or kill.

Core principle: An ad's job depends on where it lives in the funnel. A top-of-funnel ad that generates massive engagement but zero direct purchases is doing its job — it's filling your retargeting pools. Killing it because "ROAS is low" starves the funnel. Conversely, a bottom-of-funnel retargeting ad with zero engagement but strong ROAS is also doing its job. Judge each ad by the right metric for its role.

The 4 Buckets

1. Winners

High purchases, strong ROAS. The money-makers.
Signals:
Purchases ≥ 3 in last 7 days
ROAS ≥ 2.0x (prospecting) or ≥ 4.0x (retargeting)
CPA at or below target
CTR stable or improving WoW
Action: SCALE. Increase budget 10-20% every 3-4 days. Produce 2-3 variations of winning hook/format to extend lifespan. Move to main scaling campaign if still in testing.

2. Engagement Drivers (Top-of-Funnel Fuel)

High engagement, low direct purchases. Fills the remarketing pool.
Signals:
Shares ≥ 10 or Saves ≥ 15 in last 7 days
Post engagement rate ≥ 3% (likes + comments + shares / reach)
Video: ThruPlay rate ≥ 15% or 3-second view rate ≥ 50%
Low or zero direct purchases — and that's okay
Audience growth: driving profile visits, follows, page engagement
Action: KEEP RUNNING. Do NOT kill these based on ROAS alone. They are feeding the remarketing pools (video viewers, engagers, page visitors) that your Winners then close. Monitor the downstream effect: are your retargeting pools growing? If yes, the Engagement Driver is doing its job. Budget these separately — they are an investment in future conversions, not a direct return line item.

3. Underperformers

Low engagement AND low conversions. Burning cash for nothing.
Signals:
CTR < 0.5% after 5,000+ impressions
Zero purchases after spend ≥ 3x target CPA
Shares + Saves < 5 total
Video: 3-second view rate < 25%
No engagement momentum — flat or declining
Action: KILL. Pause immediately. Don't iterate on Underperformer — the hook failed. Analyze why (wrong audience? weak opening? unclear value prop?) and apply learnings to the next creative batch. Move budget to Converters or Engagement Drivers.

4. Fatiguing Creative

Were Winners or Engagement Drivers, now declining. Creative fatigue is setting in.
Signals:
Frequency ≥ 5 (prospecting) or ≥ 8 (retargeting)
CTR declining ≥ 20% WoW for 2+ consecutive weeks
CPA rising ≥ 30% above its historical average
Engagement metrics (shares, saves) dropping off
CPM increasing without corresponding performance lift
Action: ROTATE. Don't wait until it dies — rotate proactively. Produce a variation with a new hook but same winning format/angle. Pause the original, launch the variation. If the variation also fades quickly, the audience is saturated — change the audience, not just the creative.

How Engagement Drivers Feed Winners: The Remarketing Flywheel

The Funnel Connection
ENGAGEMENT DRIVER (Top of Funnel)
Viral-style content, athlete stories, product education, lifestyle videos. Gets shared, saved, watched.

Creates these audiences:
  • Video viewers (25%/50%/75%/95% watched)
  • Post engagers (liked, commented, shared)
  • Page/profile visitors
  • Post savers (high-intent signal)
  • Website visitors (if clicks through)
→
WINNER (Retargeting)
Product-focused ads, offers, social proof, urgency. Targets the warm audiences that Engagement Drivers created.

Retargets these people:
  • 75%+ video viewers (high intent — watched most of the content)
  • Post engagers from last 30 days
  • Savers (strongest organic intent signal)
  • Website visitors who didn't purchase
  • Cart abandoners
Kill the Engagement Driver → Retargeting pools shrink → Winner CPA rises → Everything gets more expensive.
This is why you never judge an Engagement Driver by its direct ROAS. Its value shows up in the Winner's performance.

Creating Engagement Drivers: Hook Framework (from CDE Knowledge Base)

When Jackie needs to create new top-of-funnel creative, use these hook categories from the CDE Video Content KB. Remember: every hook must align visual + text + spoken elements. If the viewer sees one thing, reads another, and hears a third — the hook fails.

Storytelling Hooks

Transformation narratives. Best for athlete stories, brand moments.

"This is the story of how I went from [problem] to [solution]"
"I thought I knew everything about [training], but I was so wrong"
"If I had to start [fitness journey] all over again"
"I almost gave up... until this happened"

Scroll-Stopping Hooks

Curiosity gaps and urgency. Best for product reveals, feature highlights.

"Here's what no one tells you about [workout gear]"
"Watch this before you waste money on [cheap shorts]"
"The secret to [training comfort] that no one's sharing"
"This changes everything about [workout apparel]"

Negative / Counter-Narrative Hooks

Debunking and warnings. Best for competitive positioning.

"The biggest mistake I see with [workout shorts]"
"Stop believing this lie about [athletic wear]"
"If you keep wearing [cheap gear], you'll regret it"
"Here's why your [current shorts] are holding you back"

Testing Priority (from Meta KB)

Test variables in this order for maximum signal.

1. Hook (first 3 sec) — 30-50% CTR lift
2. Format (UGC vs polished vs carousel) — 20-40% lift
3. Offer (discount vs free shipping vs bonus) — 15-30% lift
4. CTA ("Shop Now" vs "Learn More") — 5-15% lift
5. Angle (feature vs benefit vs emotional) — 20-40% lift

Test one variable at a time. Min 3 variations per test. Run 7-14 days.

Scaling Winners: The Graduation Ad Set (from CDE Meta KB)

When a Winner wins in testing but gets zero spend after moving to the scaling campaign, use this technique before giving up on it.

The Problem You test 4 creatives. All 4 win. You move them to scaling. Meta picks its favorite and starves the other 3 at $10-20/day. Most media buyers kill those ads after a week. Don't.
The Fix — Graduation Ad Set 1. Create a dedicated ad set inside the scaling campaign for "new graduates" (proven test winners not getting spend)
2. Set a minimum spend limit at 2-3x your target CPA ($100-150 for TT) on this ad set
3. This forces Meta to deliver on those ads instead of starving them
4. Once they start generating purchases, Meta begins pushing real spend organically
5. Move to main scaling ad set once proven, or let CBO redistribute

When to cut: If an ad still can't convert after spending 5-6x CPA ($250-300) in the graduation ad set, it doesn't work in this campaign context. Cut it.

Devesh owns this process. Jackie flags which test winners to graduate. Devesh sets up the ad set and monitors spend.

Metric Glossary

Quick reference for every metric used in this spec.

Definitions
Metric Formula Notes
Gross Revenue Shopify gross sales Includes shipping + tax
Net Revenue Gross − discounts − returns − shipping − tax Proxy for net demand / net sales
Marketing Spend (Total) All media + Affiliate + Ambassador + Agency Fees + Other The denominator for ROMS
Gross ROMS Gross Revenue / Total Marketing Spend Business-level efficiency. Uses Shopify revenue, not platform-reported.
Net ROMS Net Revenue / Total Marketing Spend The metric that matters most. "Are we making money?"
ROAS Platform-reported Revenue / Platform Media Spend Channel-level metric. Used for in-platform optimization, not business truth.
pCPA Total Marketing Spend (media + agency fees + affiliate + ambassador + other) / New Customers (Shopify) Fully-loaded proxy for first-purchase break-even. Target: ≤$50. Includes ALL marketing costs, not just media.
New Customer Shopify order where customer.orders_count = 1 First-time purchaser on order date.
New Customer % New Customers / Total Orders × 100 Currently ~34% of orders are new customers.
AOV Revenue / Orders Currently ~$140 gross, $147 net per order.
WoW Delta (This Week − Last Week) / Last Week × 100 Week-over-week change.
vs. 5 Wk Avg (This Week − 5 Week Avg) / 5 Week Avg × 100 Signal vs. noise — is this week unusual or trending?
