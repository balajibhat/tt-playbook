Weekly Client Call: Prep Checklist

Run through this before every Tuesday call with Patrick, Lindsay, and Jay. The goal is to never be caught off guard by a question. If you can answer "what happened, why, and what we did about it" for every metric, you're prepared.

Pre-Call Checklist (30 min before call)
# Item Who Preps What to Have Ready
1 pCPA story Devesh What was blended pCPA this week? What was Evergreen pCPA? If blended is above $50, have the Evergreen vs. Experimental breakdown ready. Know which days spiked and why.
2 Revenue & ROMS Devesh Gross and net revenue WoW. Net ROMS trend. If revenue is down, know if it's a traffic problem (less spend/clicks) or a conversion problem (same traffic, fewer sales).
3 New customer count Devesh How many new customers this week vs. last week? New customer % of total orders. If it dropped, is it because total orders went up (good) or new customers went down (investigate)?
4 Budget pacing Devesh Are we on track for monthly spend goal? Over or under? Which campaigns are over/under-delivering? Any budget reallocation recommendations?
5 Campaign bucket highlights Devesh + Jackie One sentence per bucket: what happened, any action taken. Lead with non-brand and shopping (Patrick cares most). Keep branded short unless there's an issue.
6 Experimental update Devesh + Jackie Snap / X status. What % of spend. Is pCPA trending down? Any creative learnings from Jackie? Any budget adjustments from Devesh?
7 Creative & content update Jackie Any new creative launched? What's in the pipeline? Demand Gen audience reads — Runners vs. Hybrid. Any UGC or horizontal video status from Patrick's team?
8 Upcoming calendar items Jackie What's Patrick's team pushing this week (new product drops, emails, editorial moments)? Do we need paid creative to support any of it? Flag any supplemental feed updates needed for new products.
9 Inventory / stock check Both Any OOS issues on hero products (Interval shorts, Five Pocket pant)? Should we suppress or deprioritize anything in campaigns? Lindsay/Patrick sometimes share inventory updates — track these.
The $50 pCPA question will come up every week. Patrick and Jay have said this explicitly. Always be ready with: (1) the blended number, (2) the Evergreen number, (3) why the blended is higher (experimental), and (4) what you did about it. If Evergreen pCPA is also above $50, that's the real problem — diagnose immediately and have a plan before the call.
What Patrick likes to hear: Transparency about what's working and what isn't, proactive optimization (don't wait for him to ask), context on why metrics moved (not just the number), and creative/content collaboration with his team. He's excited about the Demand Gen restructure audience reads and Shopping feed fix. Lead with those wins when ready.
